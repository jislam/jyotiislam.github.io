I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Georgia State University. My research interests include medical image analysis, deep learning, computer vision, machine learning, mHealth, and software engineering. Currently, my work focuses on developing efficient Alzheimer's disease diagnosis framework using 3D brain MRI. I am a Brain and Behavior Fellow of GSU.

I joined GSU at fall 2014. Before that, I was a Senior Software Engineer in Samsung R&D Institute Bangladesh. At Samsung R&D, I worked to develop an Automated Test Tool for Android application. Besides I worked in the S-voice and media-player app developer team.

Before joining Samsung R&D Institute, I was a Senior Software Engineer at Together Initiatives Limited. I worked in developing several enterprise applications and worked extensively in J2EE environment. Besides, I was involved in mobile health research based software development. Along with my team, I worked for developing "MHealth: Empowering CHWs to Improve Maternal, Newborn and Child Health" for BRAC.

I obtained my Bachelors and Masters degree in Computer Science and Engineering from University of Dhaka, Bangladesh.
